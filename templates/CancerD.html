Cancer develops when abnormal cells grow rapidly.

What is Cancer?

Cancer is a disease in which cells in the body begin growing uncontrollably. Normally, cells grow, divide, and die in an organized way. Cancer disrupts this process.

These abnormal cells may form tumors, damage nearby tissues, and sometimes spread to other parts of the body through the blood or lymph system.

There are many different types of cancer, including breast cancer, lung cancer, skin cancer, blood cancer, and colorectal cancer.

Common Symptoms

Cancer symptoms vary depending on the type and stage.

Unexplained Weight Loss
Persistent Fatigue
Chronic Pain
Persistent Cough
Lumps or Swelling
Changes in Skin
⚠ Persistent or unusual symptoms should be medically evaluated as early as possible.

How Cancer Develops

Cancer develops through several biological stages.

1

Cell Mutation

DNA damage causes abnormal changes in cells.

2

Rapid Growth

Abnormal cells begin multiplying uncontrollably.

3

Tumor Formation

Groups of abnormal cells form tumors.

4

Possible Spread

Cancer cells may spread to other body areas.

Causes & Risk Factors

Cancer may develop due to environmental, genetic, and lifestyle-related factors.

Smoking & Tobacco

Tobacco use significantly increases cancer risk.

Genetics

Family history may increase susceptibility to certain cancers.

Radiation & Pollution

Environmental exposure can damage healthy cells.

Types of Cancer

Cancer can affect almost any part of the body. Different cancers behave differently and require specific treatment approaches.

Common categories include carcinoma, leukemia, lymphoma, melanoma, and sarcoma.

Some cancers grow slowly while others spread rapidly, making early diagnosis extremely important.

Treatment Options

Cancer treatment depends on the type, stage, and overall patient health.

Chemotherapy

Medicines are used to destroy cancer cells.

Radiation Therapy

High-energy radiation targets cancerous tissues.

Surgery

Tumors may be surgically removed when possible.

Prevention & Healthy Habits

Healthy lifestyle choices may help reduce cancer risk.

✔ Avoid tobacco products
✔ Maintain a healthy diet
✔ Exercise regularly
✔ Protect skin from UV exposure
✔ Attend regular screenings
✔ Limit alcohol consumption

Frequently Asked Questions

Can cancer be cured?

Some cancers can be successfully treated, especially when detected early.

Is cancer hereditary?

Certain cancers may be linked to inherited genetic factors.

Does lifestyle affect cancer risk?

Smoking, diet, exercise, and environmental exposure all influence cancer risk.
