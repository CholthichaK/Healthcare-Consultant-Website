Patient Guidance

Prevention & Care

Simple daily habits and clear care steps to help you stay healthier, manage common symptoms, and know when to seek medical support.

Emergency Signs

Hand Washing

Wash hands often with soap, especially before eating and after coughing, sneezing, or going outside.

Healthy Eating

Choose fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and limit sugary drinks.

Exercise

Aim for light movement daily, such as walking, stretching, or simple home exercises.

Sleep & Hydration

Get enough rest and drink water regularly to support recovery and energy.

Vaccination Reminders

Keep routine vaccines updated and ask a doctor which vaccines are right for you.

Fever

Rest, drink fluids, wear light clothing, and monitor temperature. Avoid taking medicine without proper guidance.

Cough or Cold

Drink warm fluids, rest, and avoid close contact with others if symptoms may be infectious.

When to Rest

Rest when symptoms are strong, when you feel weak, or when sleep is poor.

When to Drink More

Increase fluids during fever, sweating, vomiting, diarrhea, or hot weather.

High Fever

Seek help if fever is very high, lasts more than three days, or comes with severe symptoms.

Chest Pain

Chest pain should be checked quickly, especially with sweating, nausea, or shortness of breath.

Breathing Difficulty

Get urgent care if breathing becomes hard, noisy, painful, or unusually fast.

Long-lasting Symptoms

Book an appointment if symptoms do not improve or keep returning.

Children

Watch hydration, fever, breathing, and behavior changes closely.

Elderly People

Pay attention to weakness, confusion, falls, appetite changes, and medication side effects.

Chronic Disease Patients

Follow regular medication plans and check symptoms early.

Pregnant People

Contact a healthcare provider for unusual pain, bleeding, swelling, fever, or reduced movement.

Emergency Warning Signs

Seek urgent medical care for severe chest pain, serious breathing difficulty, fainting, stroke-like symptoms, severe allergic reaction, heavy bleeding, or sudden confusion.
