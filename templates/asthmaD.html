Chronic Condition

Asthma is a long-term disease affecting the airways.

Breathing Difficulty

Manageable

Symptoms can often be controlled with treatment.

What is Asthma?

Asthma is a condition where the airways inside the lungs become swollen, sensitive, and narrow. This makes it difficult for air to move in and out of the lungs properly.

People with asthma may experience wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Symptoms can vary from mild discomfort to severe breathing emergencies.

Asthma attacks are often triggered by allergens, smoke, dust, exercise, cold air, or respiratory infections.

Asthma symptoms can vary depending on severity and triggers.

Wheezing

Breathing Difficulty
Fatigue
⚠ Severe breathing difficulty requires immediate medical attention.

How Asthma Attacks Happen

Asthma attacks occur through several stages inside the airways.

1

Trigger Exposure

Dust, smoke, pollen, or allergens enter the lungs.

2

Inflammation

Airways become swollen and irritated.

3

Muscle Tightening

Airway muscles tighten and reduce airflow.

4

Breathing Difficulty

Breathing becomes harder and symptoms appear.

Causes & Triggers

Multiple environmental and lifestyle factors can trigger asthma.

Environmental Factors

Pollution, pollen, dust, smoke, and weather changes can trigger asthma symptoms.

Medical Conditions

Allergies and respiratory infections may worsen asthma.

Lifestyle Triggers

Stress, strong smells, and physical activity may trigger breathing problems.

Types of Asthma

Asthma can affect people differently depending on age, triggers, and severity levels.

Common forms include allergic asthma, exercise-induced asthma, childhood asthma, and occupational asthma caused by workplace exposure.

Understanding the specific type of asthma helps doctors recommend effective treatment plans.

Treatment Options

Asthma treatments focus on controlling symptoms and preventing attacks.

Quick Relief Inhalers

Help open the airways quickly during asthma attacks.

Long-Term Medication

Reduces inflammation and prevents flare-ups.

Lifestyle Management

Avoiding triggers and maintaining healthy habits improves control.

Prevention & Self-Care

Daily preventive habits can reduce asthma complications.

✔ Avoid smoke exposure
✔ Keep inhalers nearby
✔ Monitor breathing regularly
✔ Maintain clean indoor air
✔ Exercise carefully
✔ Visit doctors regularly

Frequently Asked Questions

Can asthma be cured?

Asthma usually cannot be cured completely, but symptoms can be controlled effectively.

Is asthma hereditary?

Genetics may increase the risk of developing asthma.

Can exercise trigger asthma?

Some people experience exercise-induced asthma symptoms.
