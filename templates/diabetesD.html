Diabetes Quick Facts

Important information about diabetes and blood sugar health.

Chronic Disease

Diabetes is a long-term condition affecting blood sugar levels.

Insulin Related

The body cannot properly produce or use insulin.

Manageable

Healthy habits and medication help control diabetes.

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar levels due to problems with insulin production or insulin usage.

Insulin is a hormone that helps move sugar from the blood into cells for energy. When insulin does not work properly, blood sugar levels become too high.

If left unmanaged, diabetes can lead to serious health complications affecting the heart, kidneys, nerves, eyes, and other organs.

Common Symptoms

Diabetes symptoms may develop gradually or suddenly.

Frequent Urination
Increased Thirst
Extreme Hunger
Fatigue
Blurred Vision
Slow Healing Wounds
⚠ High blood sugar levels should be monitored immediately to prevent complications.

How Diabetes Affects the Body

Diabetes develops through several stages inside the body.

1

Insulin Problem

The body cannot properly produce or use insulin.

2

High Blood Sugar

Sugar remains in the bloodstream instead of entering cells.

3

Energy Imbalance

Cells do not receive enough glucose for proper energy.

4

Health Complications

Long-term damage can affect major organs and nerves.

Causes & Risk Factors

Several lifestyle and genetic factors increase diabetes risk.

Genetics

Family history may increase the likelihood of diabetes.

Unhealthy Lifestyle

Poor diet and lack of exercise can contribute to diabetes.

Obesity

Excess body weight increases insulin resistance.

Types of Diabetes

Diabetes exists in different forms depending on insulin production and blood sugar regulation.

Type 1 diabetes occurs when the immune system attacks insulin-producing cells. Type 2 diabetes develops when the body becomes resistant to insulin.

Gestational diabetes may occur during pregnancy and requires proper medical monitoring.

Treatment Options

Diabetes management focuses on maintaining healthy blood sugar levels.

Insulin Therapy

Insulin injections help regulate blood sugar levels.

Medication

Medicines help improve insulin function and glucose control.

Healthy Lifestyle

Diet and exercise are essential for diabetes management.

Prevention & Self-Care

Healthy daily habits help reduce diabetes complications.

✔ Maintain a healthy diet
✔ Exercise regularly
✔ Monitor blood sugar levels
✔ Maintain healthy body weight
✔ Attend regular checkups
✔ Follow prescribed treatments

Frequently Asked Questions

Can diabetes be cured?

Diabetes usually cannot be completely cured, but it can often be managed successfully.

Is diabetes hereditary?

Family history may increase the risk of developing diabetes.

Can exercise help diabetes?

Regular physical activity helps improve blood sugar control.
